# Wombo Law

## Wombo Law #1

A wombo is not a concatenation. It is a merger. Each root must surrender a syllable, a meaning, or a boundary to create something that neither word could be alone.

No sacrifice, no wombo.

## Valid sacrifice types

- **Phonemic sacrifice:** a root loses sounds, letters, syllables, or obvious surface form.
- **Boundary sacrifice:** a root loses independence and becomes a bound morpheme inside the new word.
- **Semantic sacrifice:** a root keeps most of its form but gives up its original context, dignity, or ordinary meaning.
- **Lineage sacrifice:** a prior wombo becomes a morpheme inside a newer wombo.

## Institutional process

1. Proposed
2. Validated under Wombo Law #1
3. Codified
4. Challenged, if necessary
5. Falsified or overruled, if the corpus demands it

The law is serious. The court is not.
