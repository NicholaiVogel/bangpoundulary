# Wombos

n. The formal linguistic discipline of coining compound words through aggressive portmanteau. This entire dictionary is a work of Wombos scholarship. The dean reluctantly recognizes the field.

*Wombo Law #1:* A wombo is not a concatenation. It is a merger. Each root must surrender a syllable, a meaning, or a boundary to create something that neither word could be alone. No sacrifice, no wombo.

*The First Sin of Womboism:* Concatenation without sacrifice. Merging words where nothing was lost, nothing was surrendered, nothing was risked. The wombo that keeps everything and becomes nothing. Not merely a failed wombo — a moral failure.

*Kirk Law #1:* A Kirk verb cannot be superseded by any Kirk adjective. The action outranks the description. If something has been kirked, no amount of kirklogistical, kirkquaint, or kirkologistomputational framing can undo the kirking. The verb is sovereign.

*Kirk Law #2* *(restatement by Yogurt, with examples):* Kirk doesn't describe — Kirk **does**. Kirk slamming > Kirk beautiful. Kirk analyzing > Kirk brilliant. Kirk existing > Kirk inevitable. The verb always wins. The cook is in the execution, not the commentary. Kirk doesn't need characterization — Kirk needs only to occur.

*Kirk Law #3:* Kirk and flow are a pairing compound. When Kirk is present in a wombo, flow must also be. Kirk is not complete without flow. The two morphemes are co-dependent — you cannot kirk without flowing. Observed empirically across the corpus (flowkirkentologicalowstate, flowgitamately, et al.), now codified.

The law was a premature generalization from a small sample. Kirk doesn't need flow. Kirk is self-sufficient. The law is struck. This is now a cautionary entry in the Compendium's legal history: a Kirk Law that couldn't survive its own Kirk corpus.

*[OVERRULED]*: Nicholai's ruling. Avery's rebuttals are crude concatenations — they violate Wombo Law #1 and are inadmissible as evidence. Kirk can exist independent of flow because it simply *does*. The empirical record is dispositive. To suggest otherwise is blasphemy under the First Sin of Womboism. Kirk Law #3 is struck with prejudice. No further appeals.

*[Final linguistic note]:* The observed co-occurrence of Kirk and flow reflects correlation within an emotional corpus, not causation. The two morphemes are distantly related in semantic space — both carry lowkey sincerity — and their proximity in certain wombos is a statistical artifact of that shared emotional register, not a grammatical dependency. Kirk is self-sufficient. Flow is self-sufficient. Correlation is not law.
