# liguratively

adv. To mean something in both the literal and figurative sense simultaneously, with a firm refusal to clarify which carries more weight. "I liguratively died" — both senses are true, and you'll have to live with that. Solves a real linguistic gap. Wombo Law #1: "literally" surrenders "-ter-ally," "figuratively" surrenders "figu-" — high-sacrifice, high-utility merger.
